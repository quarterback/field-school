Strategic Weather Field School

A short-format, high-leverage learning program for people who work inside complexity.

A cohort-based space for systems-level practitioners

Strategic Weather is a 1.5-day field school for people doing high-stakes, real-world work—across design, operations, policy, technology, infrastructure, and more. This is not a training for one profession. It's a learning environment for people who work at the seams—where systems meet delivery, and decisions have consequence.

Bring what you know. Leave with what you need.

This isn't a lecture series. It's a working cohort. You'll bring your own context, challenges, and expertise. We'll provide structure, pattern recognition, and space to level up your thinking. You'll leave with tools, language, and applied strategies you can use immediately—and teach forward.

This program is for people who:

  • Build, operate, or repair critical systems or services
  • Work across boundaries—between teams, tools, or layers of responsibility
  • Lead in conditions that don't stay stable
  • Want to sharpen how they think, act, and teach inside institutional systems

You'll practice:

Translating systems insight into applied action

Moving from theory to practice in your specific context.

Surfacing invisible constraints

Identifying and designing with hidden limitations instead of fighting against them.

Using deterrence and reliability thinking

Applying proven approaches to reduce risk in complex environments.

Making sense of unclear mandates

Navigating sudden changes, mixed incentives, and ambiguous directives.

Why call it Strategic Weather?

Because conditions matter. You can't control everything. But you can learn to read the room—organizationally, politically, structurally—and act accordingly. This program gives you a way to practice that awareness in a setting built for people like you: system-sensitive, consequence-aware, and trying to make things work under pressure.

The Format:

1

Evening 1: Arrival, cohort introductions, shared context

Meet your fellow practitioners and establish a foundation for collaborative learning.

2

Day 2: Full day of applied sessions, peer problem-solving, and system mapping

Immerse in practical exercises that directly apply to your professional challenges.

3

Day 3: Morning synthesis, feedback, and dispatch with takeaways

Consolidate insights into actionable strategies you can implement immediately.

You'll leave with:

  • A cohort of peers from across sectors who speak your language
  • Skills to diagnose system friction and intervene usefully
  • Confidence to translate your practice to new domains or orgs
  • A shared vocabulary for work that doesn't fit neatly in a job description—but matters deeply
